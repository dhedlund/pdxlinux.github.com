There are several ways that PLUG members may communicate with one another, both asynchronously and synchronously. Each method has it's unique features and benefits. Some are public forums, and others are for private messages between two individuals. Please keep in mind that, just as in "real life" face to face conversation, it is imortant to be polite to your fellow netizens!

eMail Discussion Broadcast Lists

Quick access links:
MailMan
Subscribe, unsubscribe, and manage your subscription or list.
Lurker
Search or browse the eMail list archives with your HTML viewer.

We use GNU Mailman to manage our eMail lists, so there is a web interface you may use to subscribe, unsubscribe, or manage your account there. In order to post to a list, one simply sends eMail to listname@lists.pdxlinux.org. The mail must be sent from a subscribed list member's eMail address for it to be automatically posted to a list. If you are not subscribed, it will hold your message, awaiting the listmaster's approval. To get around this restriction, you may subscribe more than one eMail address if you like, and then use the account management interface to set one or more of them to non-delivery mode. This way, you will be allowed to post from multiple addresses, but will only get list mail sent to one of them. Other than the subscribed-members only restriction, the messages are not censored or screened by human eyes before they are broadcast to all subscribers and permanently stored in the archive. However, they are scanned by a virus and UCE (Unsolicited Commercial Email, aka "Spam", cf. Monty Python) detector, and hopefully rejected if they contain anything evil.

The eMail discussion list archives are accessible to the public — there is an eMail Archive browse and search interface online. It will also allow you to reply to messages there by clicking a link, provided your web browser is configured to allow it. The same rules pertaining to posting by subscribed members only still apply, of course. Feel free to comment even if you are not a member — provided you are on-topic, the message will likely be allowed to post.

Internet Relay Chat channel

If you point your favorite IRC client at irc.freenode.net, you'll find many many channels where Logiciel libre users and developers gather to discuss programming and systems administration issues. Some folks from PLUG have formed a channel there, called #orlug. Feel free to join us anytime you like!

Jabber Instant Messaging

PLUG members may connect to "jabber.pdxlinux.org" with a jabber client. We do not presently have a jabber directory, and the few of us on there now are unchecking the "register with server" box in Gaim.

Your username there will be "yourname@jabber.pdxlinux.org". Though it looks like an email address, it is really not one, so don't try to send eMail there. Please try to use the same username that you use for your primary PLUG list subscription eMail address. This will make it a lot easier for others to find you via jabber.pdxlinux.org, which is kind of the point, isn't it?
